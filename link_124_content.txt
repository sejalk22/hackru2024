The School of Arts and Sciences (SAS) is the liberal arts school of Rutgers University, a globally engaged teaching and research institution that is preparing the next generation of leaders in an array of industries, fields, and professions.
SAS is home to nationally ranked programs in mathematics, computer science, English, and history and to pioneering research facilities like the Center for Cognitive Science and the Keck Center for Collaborative Neuroscience.
With our top scholars, renowned programs, and state-of-the art facilities, our graduates go on to achieve prominence in medicine, the arts, law, education, technology, academia, the social sciences, public service, and many other areas.
Our campus is located less than 30 miles outside New York City in the prominent and prosperous Northeastern hub of the United States, a center of finance, education, technology, and biotech.
