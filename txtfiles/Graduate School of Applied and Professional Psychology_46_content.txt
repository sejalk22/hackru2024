Dr. Jennifer Foster is directing MTSS programs at GSAPP and making an impact



Dr. Jennifer Foster (School PsyD, 2010) is an Asst. Teaching Prof., School Psychology Dept. and Director of Multi-Tiered Systems of Support (MTSS) online programs at GSAPP. MTSS is a compelling framework for providing comprehensive academic and behavioral health services for K-12 students. Training in MTSS is more essential than ever for Teachers, Academic Coaches, School Counselors, School Psychologists, Child Study Team Members, Administrators and other professionals to efficiently and effectively address evolving student needs.












Dr. Lori Guerriero is leading the way for Transforming School Climates in NJ



Dr. Lori Guerriero, an Organizational Psychologist (PsyD, 2004), co-manages the School Climate Transformation Project (SCTP), a partnership between GSAPP and the NJDOE. It supports K-12 public school districts in NJ, promoting data-driven processes for systemic change and positive climates. The SCTP developed the NJ School Climate Improvement (NJ SCI) Survey, administered through the free NJ SCI Platform. This platform offers data tools, planning resources, and support services for effective leadership and continuous improvement.












Dr. Elena Ragusa appointed CDC faculty partner



Dr. Elena Ragusa, (Organizational PsyD, 2010)¬†was appointed as a faculty partner to evaluate the CDC federally-funded Healthcare-Associated Infections and Antimicrobial Resistance (HAI/AR) Education and Leadership Program (HELP)












Dr. Vanessa Ramirez honored as ABCT 2023 Champion



Dr. Vanessa Ramirez (Clinical PsyD, 2011) was honored as a 2023 Champion by the Association for Behavioral and Cognitive Therapies (ABCT)! This award recognizes outstanding individuals who have shown exceptional dedication, influence, and social impact through the promotion of evidence-based psychological interventions, and who have thereby advanced the mission of ABCT.

Teacher stress and supports, classroom practices and student outcomes in high poverty urban elementary schools



Dr. Amanda Elliot, (School PsyD, 2021) served as PI on a study published in the journal, Psychology in Schools (Volume 61, Issue 1).¬†This study examined teacher competence and contextual factors associated with teacher-reported stress in low-income urban elementary schools.
