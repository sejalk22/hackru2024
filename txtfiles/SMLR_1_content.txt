  Our Academic Departments 

 






Two Perspectives on the Changing World of Work
Rutgers School of Management and Labor Relations (SMLR) is the leading source of expertise on the world of work, building effective and sustainable organizations, and the changing employment relationship. At SMLR, you will become a member of a global community that supports academic curiosity and can provide you with the critical skills needed to shape the world of work. Are you ready to take the next step?

 







 


          Explore Human Resource Management (HRM)        


SMLR's Human Resource Management (HRM) programs educate students about the relationship between HR strategy and business success and prepare them to help shape the future of the global workplace environment. 
 




 


          Explore Labor Studies and Employment Relations (LSER)        


Work is the central reality of most people’s lives. SMLR’s Labor Studies and Employment Relations (LSER) programs educate students about issues related to work, workers, and the employment relationship.

Listen Now: A Third of Your Life, SMLR's New Podcast
Episode 10: Quakertown: A Juneteenth Labor and Migration Story, Part 1
Formerly enslaved Black Americans established their own community in Denton, Texas in the late 1800s. It grew into a socially vibrant, economically prosperous town. Danielle Phillips-Cunningham, associate professor in the Rutgers School of Management and Labor Relations, talks to Alma Clark and Dianne Randolph, who are working to preserve the town’s history.

Episode 11: Quakertown: A Juneteenth Labor and Migration Story, Part 2
A devastating fire at the Frederick Douglass School in 1913 signals the growing racism that will soon threaten the entire community of Quakertown, Texas. Phillips-Cunningham talks to Clark and Randolph about the town's legacy and their work to memorialize it.

SMLR Experts In the News


Private equity is starting to share with workers, without taking a financial hit
The New York Times reports on KKR’s Ownership Works, quoting Joseph Blasi and citing data from the Institute for the Study of Employee Ownership and Profit Sharing.


As coffee shops become union battlegrounds, owners hit back
The Washington Post reports some independent coffee shops have closed to avoid a unionization effort. Rebecca Kolins Givan says the fines for doing so are “basically nonexistent.”


Workers are unionizing. How should management respond?
Fortune reports on the growing number of union campaigns. David (DK) Kryscynski says too many employers view organizing as an external threat, rather than a chance to look inward.


How Apple's Vision Pro could change the workplace, according to a labor expert
Quartz interviews Peter Rokkos about the launch of Vision Pro. He says it could bring augmented and virtual reality headsets into the mainstream and revolutionize the future of work.
